# Discrete probabilities

## Vocabulary

:::definition Random experiment

A random experiment is any experiment $\mathcal{E}$  which, depending on chance, leads to several possible results.
The universe associated with $\mathcal{E}$ is the set $\Omega$ of all possible outcomes of $\mathcal{E}$.

:::


## Uniform probability

:::proposition

The formula

$$
    \mathbb{P}(A) := \sum_{\omega \in A} p_\omega, \quad \forall A \in \mathcal{P}(\Omega),
$$

define a probability $\mathbb{P}$ on $(\Omega, \mathcal{P}(\Omega))$ as soon as the real numbers family $(p_\omega)_{\omega \in \Omega}$ verify :

1. $p_\omega \in [0, 1]$ for all $\omega \in \Omega$,
2. $\sum_{\omega \in \Omega} p_\omega = 1$

Conversely, any probability $\mathbb{P}$ on $(\Omega, \mathcal{P}(\Omega))$ is of this form: simply put

$$

p_\omega := \mathbb{P}(\{\omega\}), \quad \forall \omega \in \Omega

$$

:::

:::definition Uniform law

Let $\mathbb{P}$ be a probability on $(\Omega, \mathcal{P}(\Omega))$ with $\Omega$ finite. We say that $\mathbb{P}$ is the uniform law on $\Omega$ if all events $\omega \in \Omega$ are equiprobable : i.e.

$$

p_\omega = \frac{1}{|\Omega|}

$$

In particular we have

$$

\mathbb{P}(A) = \frac{|A|}{|\Omega|}, \quad \forall A \subset \Omega.

$$

:::
